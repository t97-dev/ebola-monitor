🇨🇩 DRC — Ituri Province ACTIVE OUTBREAK
May 20
PHECS Africa CDC declares continental health emergency. ~395 suspected cases and 106+ deaths. Outbreak now confirmed in 11 health zones across Ituri and Nord-Kivu provinces. Director-General Kaseya: "Africa's health security is indivisible."
May 19
WHO DG warns of "scale and speed" — over 500 suspected cases, 130+ deaths. MSF dispatches 8 tons of supplies (PPE, medicines, generators, solar panels) from Kinshasa to Bunia. US deploys disaster assistance team to Kinshasa.
May 18
US/DHS Title 42 entry restrictions implemented. American citizen tests positive in DRC; evacuation initiated. Workers renovating Ebola isolation and observation sites in Goma. Temperature screening checkpoints established at hospitals.
May 17
PHEIC WHO declares Public Health Emergency of International Concern — highest alert level. DRC cabinet emergency session on outbreak. International response escalates dramatically.
May 16
Africa CDC high-level coordination meeting: 130+ participants including DRC, Uganda, South Sudan, WHO, UNICEF, FAO, WFP, IOM, OCHA, pharma companies, and donors. 8 lab-confirmed cases, 246 suspected, 80 deaths at this point.
May 15
DRC MoH officially confirms outbreak. Bundibugyo strain — no approved vaccine or treatment. Traced to Mongbwalu HZ, a gold-mining hub. CDC issues Level 3 Travel Notice.
May 2
Cluster of severe illness identified at Bunia hospital, affecting healthcare workers. Initial tests false-negative (wrong strain kits). WHO alerted.
Apr 24
Index case: health worker in Mongbwalu develops symptoms. Mongbwalu is a high-traffic gold mining area with significant regional connectivity.
🇺🇬 Uganda 2 CONFIRMED
May 20
Authorities intensify contact tracing in Kampala. Busunga border crossing (DRC–Uganda) now has Ebola emergency posters and active screening. EAC formally urges all partner states to heighten surveillance and cross-border coordination.
May 19
State Department issues Level 4 "Do Not Travel" advisory for Uganda. US Embassy in Kampala remains open. Temperature screening active at Kyeshero Hospital. MSF confirms readiness to support Uganda MoH.
May 18
Goma (DRC–Uganda border area) steps up isolation site construction. Visitor temperature checks at hospital entry points in Goma and border zones. Contact tracing expanded.
May 16
Second confirmed case in Kampala — no apparent link to first. Both cases confirmed within 24 hours, raising concerns. Uganda MoH contact tracing intensified across the capital.
May 15
First confirmed case: 59-year-old Congolese man, died May 14 in Kampala. Imported from DRC. Uganda MoH triggers emergency protocol. CDC issues Level 1 Travel Health Notice.
Risk
Ituri Province DRC is less than 500km from Uganda. High-volume cross-border movement via Busunga/Bundibugyo crossing. MSF preparing Ebola Treatment Centre with Uganda MoH in Mongbwalu area.

South Sudan: High Risk — No Confirmed Cases (as of May 21)
South Sudan shares a direct border with Ituri Province, DRC — the epicentre of this outbreak. Significant cross-border population movement and 11,000+ South Sudanese refugees currently in Ituri Province create serious transmission risk. South Sudan has no prior Ebola outbreak experience and relies heavily on international support. All WHO, Africa CDC, UNICEF, and partner preparedness protocols have been activated.

Latest updates · South Sudan
May 20
Africa CDC Director-General explicitly: "I express support for South Sudan, which is geographically close to Ituri province, in advancing its preparedness measures." South Sudan included in continental emergency declaration framework (PHECS).
May 19
US State Dept issues Level 4 "Do Not Travel" advisory for South Sudan due to regional Ebola risk. US Embassy in Juba operational. Entry ban now applies to non-US citizens who visited South Sudan in past 21 days.
May 18
EAC partner states — including South Sudan — formally urged to heighten surveillance, strengthen emergency preparedness, and intensify cross-border coordination. Sudan (separately) tightens all border and airport controls.
May 16
South Sudan participates in Africa CDC emergency coordination meeting alongside DRC and Uganda. Focus includes cross-border surveillance at South Sudan–Ituri frontier. UNICEF coordinating supplies for ~2,000 Rwandan/Burundian refugees in Goma and 11,000 South Sudanese in Ituri.
May 15
DRC, Uganda and South Sudan jointly take "immediate actions to strengthen surveillance, preparedness and response" with Africa CDC and partners. PHEOC (Public Health Emergency Operations Centre) placed on alert status.
Actions
Current preparedness measures: WHO South Sudan country office activating border health protocols; PHEOC on alert; key DRC border crossings assessed for screening capacity; IOM monitoring population movement; UNICEF/OCHA coordinating refugee support; coordination with humanitarian actors across Western Equatoria and Torit State border zones.
Key gap
No vaccine, no treatment: Unlike the 2018–19 DRC outbreak (Zaire strain, ring-vaccination possible), the Bundibugyo strain has no approved vaccine or specific therapeutic. WHO estimates vaccine ~2 months away. South Sudan cannot deploy ring vaccination as a response strategy — containment depends entirely on surveillance, isolation, and infection control.

The outbreak originated in Mongbwalu, a gold-mining town in Ituri Province, DRC. Cases spread to Bunia and Rwampara before crossing into Uganda (Kampala). Ituri borders South Sudan directly. As of May 20, the outbreak spans 11 health zones across two DRC provinces. Juba is approximately 400km from the nearest affected health zones.
