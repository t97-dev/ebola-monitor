South Sudan: High Risk — No Confirmed Cases (May 21)
South Sudan shares a direct border with Ituri Province DRC — the outbreak epicentre. Significant cross-border movement, 11,000+ South Sudanese refugees currently in Ituri, no prior Ebola response experience, and no Bundibugyo vaccine make this the most vulnerable at-risk country. All WHO, Africa CDC, UNICEF, and partner preparedness protocols are activated. Juba is approximately 400km from the nearest affected health zones.

Key gap
No vaccine, no treatment: Unlike 2018–19 (Zaire strain, ring vaccination possible), Bundibugyo has no approved vaccine or therapeutic. WHO estimates vaccine ~2 months away at earliest. South Sudan cannot deploy ring vaccination — containment depends entirely on surveillance and isolation.
⚠ Critical accountability issue: The IRC officially warned (May 19) that US government funding cuts from March 2025 led to reduced Ituri programming from 5 to 2 coverage areas — directly weakening disease surveillance at what is now the outbreak epicentre. Before 2025, US-funded IRC work included IPC infrastructure, triage zones, handwashing stations, and surveillance systems.

Outbreak originated in Mongbwalu, a high-traffic gold mining town in Ituri Province, DRC. Spread to Bunia and Rwampara before crossing into Uganda (Kampala). Ituri borders South Sudan directly. As of May 20, the outbreak spans 11 health zones across two DRC provinces. Juba is approximately 400km from the nearest affected health zones.
